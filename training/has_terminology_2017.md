## Style Observations from HAS

### Person-Centered Language
- HAS explicitly states that "personne autiste" and "adulte autiste" were preferred over "personne avec TSA" on proposal from autistic participants
- Emphasizes "construit avec lui et non pour lui" (constructed with them, not for them)

### Key Phrases for Rights
- "droit à la non-discrimination en raison du handicap"
- "droit à une information claire compréhensible et adaptée"
- "le droit de consentir ou de refuser les soins"
- "le droit de ne pas faire l'objet de soins inappropriés"

### Recommendation Formats
HAS uses specific phrasing for recommendations:
- "Il est recommandé de..." (It is recommended to...)
- "Il convient de..." (It is appropriate to...)
- "Veiller à..." (Ensure that...)
- "S'assurer que..." (Make sure that...)
- "Favoriser..." (Promote...)
- "Privilégier..." (Prioritize...)
- "Éviter de..." (Avoid...)

### Sentence Structure
- Long, formal sentences with multiple subordinate clauses
- Frequent use of bullet points (•) for recommendations
- Section markers (►) for subsections

## Section 7: Comportements-problèmes

This section is particularly relevant to PDA translation:

### 7.1 Prévention des comportements-problèmes

Key concepts:
- Designating trained "personnes ressources" (resource persons) for challenging behaviors
- Implementing prevention and management strategies
- Training all professionals at all levels
- Establishing individualized intervention plans
- Secondary prevention through longitudinal traceability

### 7.2 Gestion des comportements-problèmes

Key concepts:
- Functional assessment of behavior
- Understanding antecedents and consequences
- De-escalation and calming strategies
- Environmental adaptation
- Avoiding inappropriate hospitalization solely for behavioral issues

## Usage Notes for Translation

When translating PDA literature into French:
1. Use HAS terminology for institutional/clinical concepts
2. "Accompagnement" is preferred in modern HAS documents over "prise en charge"
3. "Comportements-problèmes" (hyphenated) is the official term for challenging behaviors
4. "Projet d'accompagnement personnalisé" for individualized support plans
5. Identity-first language ("personne autiste") is officially sanctioned
6. Reference CIF framework when discussing functional assessments
7. Use "approche centrée sur la personne" for person-centered approach
8. "Écoute" and "empathie" are emphasized values
9. "Prévisibilité" and "anticipation" are key environmental concepts
10. "Pair-aidance" for peer support is a recognized term
